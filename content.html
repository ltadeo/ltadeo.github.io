            The “War on Drugs” campaign was started by President Rodrigo Duterte when he took office on June 30, 2016. The War on Drugs campaign is an anti-drug policy which is aimed at neutralizing illegal drug use nationwide. Effectiveness of the campaign is controversial and up for debate, but what is sure is that the campaign has made serious violations against basic human rights.

            The methods the administration uses to neutralize drug use in the country have been very questionable in terms of their ethics. According to a study done by “Human Rights Watch”, the PDEA reported 4,948 suspected drug users and dealer died during the operations from July the first, 2016 to September 30, 2018, this report however fails to identify thousands of others killed by unknown gunmen. According to PNP over 22,000 deaths since the campaign started are classified as “homicides under investigation.” Exact numbers are hard to find because the government has not disclosed any official documents regarding the “drug war.” To top this all off, according to Human Rights Watch research police have been falsifying evidence to justify unlawful killings. Even after all of this the head of drug enforcement for the Philippine National Police, Caramat, said in his own words in an interview done by Reuters “Drug supply is stil l rampant.”
